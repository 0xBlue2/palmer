Generally, Title IX requires nearly all schools and colleges/universities to prevent and investigate sex-based discrimination. Sex-based discrimination includes the things you might expect(like discrimination against pregnant people or athletes), as well as sex-based harassment.

There are three main types of sex-based harassment covered under Title IX:

[http://www.ed.gov/laws-and-policy/civil-rights-laws/frequently-asked-questions-sex-discrimination#sex-based-harassment](http://www.ed.gov/laws-and-policy/civil-rights-laws/frequently-asked-questions-sex-discrimination#sex-based-harassment)
- "hostile environment" harassment
    - severe, pervasive behavior that interferes with your right to fair/safe education
        - or behavior that __would__ reasonably interfere with that right
- "quid pro quo" harassment
    - an employee of the school "offers" aid or a benefit in exchange for sexual conduct
- sexual misconduct
    - crimes like sexual assault, stalking, and domestic violence
    - MGA also abides by policies from the USG Board of Regents, which also forbids:

        [https://policies.mga.edu/policy-manual/section-6-campus-affairs/6-5-nondiscrimination/6-5-1-sexual-misconduct/index.php](https://policies.mga.edu/policy-manual/section-6-campus-affairs/6-5-nondiscrimination/6-5-1-sexual-misconduct/index.php)
        - Knowingly transmitting an STD or HIV
        - Sexually-based bullying
        - Non-consensual distribution of photo, video, or audio of sexual activity, even if the sexual activity or capturing of the activity was consensual
